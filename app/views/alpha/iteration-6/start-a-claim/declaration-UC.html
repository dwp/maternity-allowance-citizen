If you get Universal Credit

Your Universal Credit payment will be reduced by an amount equal to your Maternity Allowance payment.

You may get an extra amount of Universal Credit for your children (whether you get Maternity Allowance of not).

Report a change on your Universal Credit account if you start getting Maternity Allowance.

What benefits you get will affect which National Insurance credits you're eligible for. If you get Universal Credit:

  • With Maternity Allowance payments you get Class 1 National Insurance credits, which help you qualify for some other benefits and State Pension
  • Without Maternity Allowance payments, you get Class 3 National Insurance credits, which count towards your State Pension only.

Payments

Your payments for Universal Credit and Maternity Allowance are received on different times of the month. Universal Credit is paid on the same date of every month. Maternity Allowance is paid every 2 or 4 weeks.

Other benefits

The other benefits that can be affected are:

  • Bereavement benefits
  • Carer's Allowance
  • Carer support payment
  • Employment Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Jobseeker's Allowance - this will stop if you get Maternity Allowance

You can use the benefit calculator to help what benefits you could get and how much you could receive.

Pregnancy-related sick leave

If you are receiving Statutory Sick Pay (SSP), New Style Employment and Support Allowance or Incapcity Benefit in this 4 week peiod, it will stop.
