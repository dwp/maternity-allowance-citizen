Apply for Maternity Allowance

Maternity Allowance are payments you can get when you take time off to have a baby.

You could get it if you:

  • are employed but cannot get Statutory Maternity Pay (SMP)
  • are self-employed
  • have recently stopped working
  • take part in unpaid work for the business of your spouse or civil partner

You can get Maternity Allowance for up to 39 weeks and can claim after the 26th week of the pregnancy.

If you delay sending your claim

If you claim later than 3 months after your entitlement starts, you may not get the full amount of Maternity Allowance.

Before you start

You'll need to upload evidence of:

  • your maternity certificate (MAT B1)
  • an SMP1 form from each employer you had during the 15th week before the week your baby is due
  • your baby's birth certificate, if applicable
  • your baby's still birth or death certificate if your baby was still born or had a neonatal death

You’ll need to have a GOV.UK One Login account. You’ll have a chance to create an account if you do not have one already.

Maternity Allowance may affect other benefits or entitlements you are getting, including Universal Credit.
