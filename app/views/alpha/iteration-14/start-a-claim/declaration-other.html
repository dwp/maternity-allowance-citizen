Effects on other benefits

The other benefits that can be affected are:

  • bereavement benefits, for example Bereavement Support Payment
  • Carer's Allowance
  • Carer Support Payment (Scotland only)
  • Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA) - this will stop if you get Maternity Allowance

You can use the benefit calculator (opens in a new tab) to help find out what benefits you could get, how much you could get and how to claim.

If you’re claiming ESA or Income Support, you may also get Sure Start Maternity Grant (SSMG) (opens in new tab). This helps with the costs of having a baby if you live in England or Wales and you or your partner are on a qualifying benefit and do not have another child under the age of 16 living in the same household.

Payments

Most benefits are paid every 2 or 4 weeks. If you’re getting any other benefits and Maternity Allowance, these may fall on different days.

If you’re employed on pregnancy-related sick leave

If you’re getting Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) on or after the 4th week before the week the baby is due, this will stop.

If you’re getting New Style Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) or Incapacity Benefit in this 4-week period, this may stop.
