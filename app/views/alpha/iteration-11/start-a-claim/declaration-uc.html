If you're claiming Universal Credit, you may also get:

  • Sure Start Maternity Grant (SSMG) (opens in new tab). This helps with the costs of having a baby if you live in England or Wales and you or your partner are on a qualifying benefit and donâ€™t have another child under the age of 16 living in the same household.
  • Best Start Grant - Pregnancy and Baby Payment (opens in new tab). This helps with the costs of having a baby if you live in Scotland
  • Healthy Start vouchers (opens in new tab) to buy healthy foods like milk or fruit if you live in England or Wales. If you are under 18, you can claim these even if you are not on a qualifying benefit.
  • Best Start Foods (opens in new tab). These are payments to help you buy healthy foods during pregnancy, if your child is under 3 and you live in Scotland

Payments

Universal Credit and Maternity Allowance are paid at different times. Universal Credit is paid monthly. Maternity Allowance is paid every 2 or 4 weeks.

You can use the benefit calculator (opens in new tab) to help find out what benefits you could get, how much you could get and how to claim.

National Insurance credits

What benefits you get will affect which National Insurance credits (opens in new tab) you're eligible for:

  • if you claim Maternity Allowance payments, you get Class 1 National Insurance credits which help you qualify for some other benefits and State Pension
  • if you claim Universal Credit only, you get Class 3 National Insurance credits, which count towards your help you qualify for State Pension only
