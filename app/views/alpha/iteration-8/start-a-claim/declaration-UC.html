You may get an extra amount of Universal Credit for your children (whether you get Maternity Allowance of not).

Report a change on your Universal Credit account if you start getting Maternity Allowance.

If you are claiming Universal Credit, you can also get:

  • Sure Start Maternity Grant. You may get this if you are claiming Maternity Allowance under the age of 18
  • Healthy Start vouchers to buy healthy foods like milk or fruit
  • Best Start Grant - Pregnancy and Baby Payment. This helps with the costs of having a baby if you live in Scotland
  • Best Start Foods. This is a payment to help you buy healthy foods during pregnancy, if your child is under 3 and you live in Scotland

If you only claim Maternity Allowance, you are not eligible for these other payments.

Payments

Universal Credit and Maternity Allowance are paid at different times. Universal Credit is paid monthly. Maternity Allowance is paid every 2 or 4 weeks.

You can use the benefit calculator to help what benefits you could get and how much you could receive.

Pregnancy-related sick leave

If you are on sick leave due to a pregnancy-related illness on or after the 4th week before the week the baby is due, your Maternity Allowance must start at any time in this 4 week period.

If you are receiving Statutory Sick Pay (SSP), New Style Employment and Support Allowance or Incapcity Benefit in this 4 week peiod, it will stop.

National Insurance Credits

What benefits you get will affect which National Insurance credits you're eligible for. If you get Universal Credit:

  • With Maternity Allowance payments you get Class 1 National Insurance credits, which help you qualify for some other benefits and State Pension
  • Without Maternity Allowance payments, you get Class 3 National Insurance credits, which count towards your State Pension only
